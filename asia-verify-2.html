Overview

Clients often ask the product design team, “How can we streamline the Billing and Subscription dashboard and simplify the user journey?” In response, we focused on showcasing AsiaVerify’s unique strengths and positioning it as a leading platform for reliable data generation. Our primary goal was to develop a clean, user-friendly subscription interface that elevates usability and highlights critical information. Guided by user research, data insights, and thoughtful critique, we identified core challenges that impact millions of users worldwide and crafted solutions to address their most pressing needs.

Background & Challenges

The legacy dashboard struggled with conveying distinct subscription states, often leaving users confused about critical details such as renewal dates, billing cycles, and available balances. Key challenges included:
Cluttered Information: Users found it difficult to quickly extract important data due to inconsistent presentation of subscription details and usage metrics.
Inconsistent Messaging: Differing subscription states (active/upgraded, exhausted balances, cancelled subscriptions) were not clearly distinguished, leading to misunderstandings about current account status and next steps.
Delayed Feedback: Users experienced frustration from slow or unclear updates following billing events, resulting in increased support queries.
Adaptability Issues: The interface needed to dynamically adjust content based on subscription type, whether annual or monthly, and the unique status of each user, from active engagement to cancellation scenarios.

Research & Discovery

UX Research

To uncover the core challenges and opportunities for improvement on the Subscription dashboard, we conducted a comprehensive user research study. We gathered feedback by directly asking users about their experiences and challenges with the current dashboard, then organized the findings by severity to identify the most pressing issues.

User Interviews: We engaged directly with users to gather insights on their experiences with the current Subscription dashboard. Feedback revealed that users struggled with locating renewal dates, understanding plan features, and interpreting messages related to exhausted balances or cancellation.
Customer Support Data: We reviewed customer support tickets to identify common billing-related issues. This analysis revealed recurring complaints about surprise charges and missed renewal notifications, highlighting critical pain points in the user journey.
Heuristic Evaluation: We conducted a usability audit based on Nielsen’s Heuristics, which uncovered significant issues with system status visibility, error prevention, and overall consistency in the dashboard design.

Key Insights

These insights underscored the necessity of an interface that not only provides real-time updates and clear navigation but also delivers personalized content that aligns with the specific subscription status of each user.

Visibility of System Status: Users need an immediate, clear snapshot of their current plan, billing cycle, and remaining balance.
Guided Actions: There is a strong desire for streamlined actions that allow users to quickly top up or upgrade their subscriptions without navigating through multiple pages.
Clear Hierarchy: Critical subscription details—such as plan cost, next billing date, and subscription expiry—must be prominently displayed to reduce user confusion.
Accessible Interactions: Current design elements, such as contrast levels and text sizes, are insufficient for some users, emphasizing the need for design adjustments that meet WCAG guidelines.

Active User Journey

To design a more intuitive Subscription dashboard, we mapped out the entire user journey to understand every interaction point and identify potential friction areas. This detailed analysis enabled us to align design decisions with user needs and ensure that each step of the billing process is both seamless and efficient.

Mapping the workflow

Entry Point: Once a payment is successfully processed, users are immediately redirected to the “My Plan Dashboard” with the Subscription History tab open by default, ensuring they are presented with the most relevant information right away.
Primary Goal: The main objective for users is to quickly and clearly view their current subscription status. This includes details such as their active plan, remaining balance, and the next billing or renewal date.
Secondary Goals: Beyond checking their status, users often need to update payment information, review historical billing data, or contact support for issues or clarifications.
Pain Points & Opportunities:

  • Navigation Challenges: Users have previously encountered difficulties locating key details such as renewal dates and specific plan features.
  • Cluttered Presentation: Critical information was often lost in a lack of clear visual hierarchy, making it hard to digest at a glance
  • Actionable Shortcuts: There is a need for streamlined processes that allow users to top-up or upgrade subscriptions without navigating through multiple pages.

Applying Gestalt and Usability Principles

We applied core Gestalt and usability principles to design a dashboard that is both visually coherent and functionally efficient, ensuring a personalized experience for both active and upgraded users. Drawing on our design documentation, we structured the “My Plan Dashboard” to adapt seamlessly to various subscription types while maintaining consistency and clarity across all elements.

  • Adaptive Information Hierarchy: By leveraging proximity and similarity, we grouped related elements—such as the Subscription Plan Breakdown, Balances Usage Tracker, and Subscription Expiry Tracker—so users can immediately grasp key information about their current plan. For example, essential details like the billing cycle, next billing date, and auto-renewal information are clearly delineated, reducing cognitive load and guiding users toward appropriate actions.
  • Consistent Visual Structure: A consistent design language was established across the dashboard. Uniform typography, color coding (green for >50%, orange for 25-50%, and red for 0-24% thresholds), and iconography create an intuitive interface. This consistency not only reinforces the brand identity but also enables users to easily interpret complex data—whether they're reviewing their subscription plan or tracking usage and expiry details.
  • Seamless Navigation and Flow: The layout supports a logical progression of tasks. Upon successful payment, users are redirected to the “My Plan Dashboard” with the Subscription History tab open by default. This intentional design choice, combined with clearly defined filters, sorting options, and navigational elements (such as pagination and refresh buttons), ensures that users can effortlessly move between sections like Subscription History and Usage History, minimizing friction and improving overall efficiency.
  • Dynamic Feedback and Interactive Affordance: Real-time updates and clear visual cues are integral to the dashboard’s design. Trackers update immediately when a report is generated or a billing event occurs, providing instant feedback. Visual indicators—such as green dots for newly updated rows and tooltips on trackers—offer context-sensitive information, helping users understand their current status and available actions without delay.
  • Accessibility and Error Prevention: Emphasis on accessible design is evident through high-contrast color schemes, legible typography, and comprehensive tooltips. These elements, along with error prevention strategies like intuitive sorting options and the inclusion of a dedicated support CTA, ensure that users of all abilities can navigate the dashboard with ease and confidence.
By applying these Gestalt and usability principles, we crafted a “My Plan Dashboard” that not only presents comprehensive subscription details in an organized manner but also provides a responsive, user-friendly experience tailored to the needs of both active and upgraded users.
